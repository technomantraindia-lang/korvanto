Frequently Asked Questions

What is bentonite?
Bentonite is a naturally occurring clay primarily composed of the mineral montmorillonite. It is known for its excellent swelling, water absorption, binding, sealing, and adsorption properties, making it suitable for applications across drilling, foundry, iron ore pelletization, civil engineering, animal feed, paper, pharmaceuticals, cosmetics, and many other industries.
What are the main types of bentonite?
The two main natural types are Sodium Bentonite and Calcium Bentonite. Depending on the intended application, bentonite may also be processed, activated, or modified to achieve specific performance characteristics required for industrial use.
What industries use bentonite?
Bentonite is widely used in: Oil & Gas Drilling Water Well Drilling Horizontal Directional Drilling (HDD) Foundries Iron Ore Pelletization Civil Engineering & Piling Animal Feed Agriculture & Fertilizers Paper & Pulp Pharmaceuticals Cosmetics Cat Litter Environmental Sealing Construction Water Treatment
What makes bentonite suitable for industrial applications?
Bentonite offers a unique combination of high swelling capacity, excellent water absorption, strong binding properties, good rheological performance, sealing capability, and high cation exchange capacity. These characteristics make it one of the most versatile industrial minerals available.
How do I choose the right bentonite grade?
The appropriate grade depends on the intended application, required performance, operating conditions, and customer specifications. Is bentonite environmentally friendly?
Bentonite is a naturally occurring mineral that is widely used in environmentally responsible applications such as sealing ponds, landfill liners, drilling fluids, and water management systems. Appropriate handling and disposal should always follow local regulations and the intended application.
Can bentonite be used in multiple industries?
Yes. Bentonite is one of the most versatile industrial minerals available. Different grades are engineered for specific industries such as drilling, foundry, iron ore pelletization, animal feed, civil engineering, paper, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and many other applications.
What factors affect bentonite performance?
Performance depends on several factors, including mineral composition, montmorillonite content, particle size, moisture, activation process, application conditions, dosage, and the specific grade selected for the intended use.

What is the difference between Sodium Bentonite and Calcium Bentonite?
Sodium bentonite is known for its high swelling capacity, superior water absorption, and excellent viscosity, making it ideal for drilling fluids, civil engineering, sealing, and environmental applications. Calcium bentonite has lower swelling characteristics but offers good adsorption and binding properties, making it suitable for applications such as animal feed, agriculture, absorbents, and certain industrial processes. The appropriate type depends on the intended application and performance requirements.
What is Montmorillonite and why is it important?
Montmorillonite is the primary clay mineral found in bentonite and is responsible for most of its valuable properties, including swelling, water absorption, binding, sealing, and cation exchange capacity (CEC). In general, a higher montmorillonite content contributes to improved performance, although the required level varies depending on the intended application and industry.
What is Activated Bentonite?
Activated bentonite is bentonite that has been chemically or thermally processed to enhance specific properties for particular applications. Acid-activated bentonite is commonly used in edible oil purification, bleaching, and chemical processing, while soda activation improves the swelling and rheological properties of calcium bentonite for applications such as drilling fluids and civil engineering. The activation method depends on the desired end use.
What is Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC) in bentonite?
Cation Exchange Capacity (CEC) is a measure of bentonite's ability to attract, hold, and exchange positively charged ions. This property contributes to its adsorption performance, binding efficiency, and interaction with water and other materials. CEC is particularly important in applications such as agriculture, animal feed, environmental remediation, and water treatment, where ion exchange plays a significant role in product performance.
How should bentonite be stored?
Bentonite should be stored in a clean, dry, and well-ventilated area, protected from moisture, rain, and contamination. Bags should be kept sealed until use and preferably stored on pallets away from direct contact with the floor. Proper storage helps preserve product quality, flowability, and performance throughout its shelf life.
