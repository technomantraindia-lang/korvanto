Shipping Policy

Last updated: 16 July 2026. How Korvanto LLP handles packaging, documentation, and export dispatch for industrial mineral shipments from India.

Export Logistics Global Dispatch Terms

Overview

This Shipping Policy explains how Korvanto LLP coordinates packaging, documents, and export dispatch for industrial mineral shipments. Final logistics details for each order are written in the confirmed quotation, proforma invoice, or sales contract.

Before Dispatch

Shipments move forward after written order confirmation covering product grade, quantity, packaging, destination, Incoterms, and payment terms. Trial loads and full container programmes can be planned based on product availability and buyer needs.

Packaging Options

Depending on the product and order, Korvanto can supply minerals in export-ready formats such as:

  • 25 kg bags
  • 50 kg bags
  • Jumbo bags / FIBC
  • Palletized loads
  • Containerized shipments
  • Bulk supply options where practical

Packaging choice should consider moisture protection, destination handling equipment, and port practices. Custom packaging can be discussed on request.

Shipping Terms

Common shipping terms include FOB, CFR/CNF, and CIF, based on mutual agreement. Port of loading, freight arrangement, insurance responsibility, and risk transfer follow the Incoterms written in the confirmed order.

Dispatch Coordination

After stock allocation and packaging, Korvanto works with logistics partners on inland movement and port dispatch. Buyers receive updates as documents and sailing details become available.

Lead times depend on grade availability, packaging, vessel schedules, documentation readiness, and destination requirements. Estimated timelines are shared at order confirmation and may change if logistics conditions change.

Export Documents

Standard export documents usually include:

  • Commercial invoice
  • Packing list
  • Bill of lading or transport document

When agreed in the order, Korvanto can also support Certificates of Analysis, Technical Data Sheets, Safety Data Sheets, and other certificates needed for the buyer’s market.

Buyer Responsibilities

Buyers are responsible for:

  • Sharing accurate destination, consignee, and import details
  • Checking local import rules, permits, and unloading capability
  • Arranging destination clearance and inland delivery when required under the agreed shipping terms
  • Inspecting goods promptly on arrival

Shortage, Damage, and Claims

Please tell Korvanto LLP in writing about any shortage, damage, or quality concern soon after arrival, ideally within 7 days of receipt at destination. Include photographs, surveyor reports if available, and the related shipping documents. Claims are reviewed under the confirmed contract and agreed shipping terms.

Delays Outside Our Control

Korvanto is not responsible for delays caused by events beyond reasonable control such as port congestion, vessel cancellation, natural events, strikes, regulatory restrictions, or sudden transport disruptions. We will share major delay updates and work with buyers on practical options when possible.
